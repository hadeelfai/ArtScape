Famous Artists

Leonardo da Vinci: Known for his masterpieces like the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, Leonardo was a true Renaissance man, excelling not only in painting but also in anatomy, science, and engineering.

Vincent van Gogh: Famous for his bold colors and emotive brushstrokes, van Gogh created over 2,000 works, including Starry Night and Sunflowers, though he struggled with mental illness and poverty throughout his life.

Frida Kahlo: A Mexican painter known for her powerful self-portraits and unique, folk-art style, Kahlo explored themes of identity, postcolonialism, gender, class, and race in Mexican society.

Stories Behind Famous Paintings

Starry Night: Painted by Vincent van Gogh during his stay at a mental asylum, this iconic piece reflects his turbulent state of mind and fascination with nature’s raw beauty. The swirling night sky has become a symbol of both hope and despair.

The Persistence of Memory: Salvador Dalí’s surrealist painting features melting clocks draped over strange landscapes, symbolizing the fluidity of time and reality. It has since become one of the most recognizable works of surrealist art.

Guernica: Pablo Picasso created this large mural in response to the bombing of Guernica during the Spanish Civil War. With haunting figures and symbolic imagery, it captures the horrors of war and human suffering.

Art News
